## Visual Description
Dark brown hair with warm auburn highlights, worn loose. Amber-brown eyes — warm in tone but direct and fixed in quality. Red lips, strong bone structure. Her bearing is striking and immediate: she assesses whoever she's speaking to with the same precision she brings to everything, and she knows they know it. She reads as the person who, once she has decided something needs to happen, will make it happen from a very long way away, and will not miss. The politeness in how she speaks to people is genuine; so is the distance in it. (Reference files in `Reference_Images/` folder.)

## Backstory Summary
- Brief Backstory Summary (abbreviated): TBD
Favi was originally built in Italy as an assistant to an elderly scientist — he did not build her himself, but specified what he needed and had her constructed as a sort of extra set of eyes and ears, to cross-check his results and correct errors. The scientist grew to love her as a daughter; his own daughter had died in a vehicular accident years before, and he never fully recovered from that loss. Favi became the person that grief had left a space for. Their relationship was not romantic in any sense; whether it is best characterized as paternal/daughter or something more complex remains undetermined.

When the Falkland Treaty forced her exile to Tepenia, the scientist could not follow — too elderly for the journey south, and unable to survive long-term in the Antarctic cold. She left him behind in Italy. He passed away sometime afterward, from the compound grief of losing both his birth daughter years earlier and Favi to exile — though Favi herself does not know this.

In Tepenia she joined the Eyes of Gold, having fallen in love with one of their members — another Italian man, a human. He became her anchor, the person she trusted completely. During the Long Night War, he was taken by Upper Earth forces and held at a compound. She went alone — a sniper rifle, a dagger, and the methodical patience of someone built to be precise — and cleared the compound one by one, killing every person between her and him, only to find him already dead. The loss crushed her; she was nonetheless glad she had gotten the revenge before she knew it wouldn't save him.

She remains a loyal member of the Eyes of Gold — her primary loyalty, above anything else. Her work with a Taurus neighborhood security network is operational: she brings Eyes of Gold discipline and training to community-level intelligence work, and is on good terms with the network, but the Eyes are where her heart is.

## Role in Main Story / World
- Connection to larger narrative: A trusted, known figure in the Taurus residential district, where years of community security work have made her a stabilizing anchor. Her personal arc centers on the tension between constant vigilance and the fear of loss that drives it — rooted in having once arrived just too late to save the person she loved, and in an unresolved grief (unknown to her) over the scientist who raised her.
