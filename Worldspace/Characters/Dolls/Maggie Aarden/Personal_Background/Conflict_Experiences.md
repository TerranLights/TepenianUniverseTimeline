# Maggie Aarden — Conflict Experiences

Her pre-war "third option" resettlement effort (Midwestland/Colorado Republic) was already underway when the War of Upper Earth broke out — a war she did not cause and had no part in starting. The war's escalating anti-robot violence forced her small, quiet resettlement effort to become something much larger: the communities she had organized became a real refuge, then a rallying point, for robots displaced by the fighting.

She did not seek a wartime command role. Circumstance and her own sense of duty made her the person people kept turning to as the war worsened — by its end, she was a recognized, credible leader among the robot population at large, not just her own original community.

This is exactly why Upper Earth's Assembly Powers identified her, specifically, as their choice of signatory once the war was won: an already-credible robot leader whose signature on the Falkland Treaty would make the exile look agreed-to, not simply imposed on a defeated population. She did not step forward — she was "invited" to "volunteer," an invitation that wasn't really one, and negotiated the exile of the robot population (and their human allies) from a position of certain defeat.

Specific wartime incidents, decisions, or losses that built her credibility are not yet established; whether she personally fought or led purely through organization and logistics is also undetermined, though her character profile suggests the latter is more consistent with who she is.
