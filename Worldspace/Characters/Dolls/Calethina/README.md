## Backstory Summary
Calethina is an artificial intelligence, not a physical Doll — she exists solely as a holographic projection with no doll body of her own. She operates one of Concordia's few still-functioning robot activation labs, where she greets, orients, and mentors newly-activated robots. She has existed since before the Long Night War and played a major role in coordinating the evacuation of Concordia's population via the Amundsen Tower into orbit.

Much of her original hardware was destroyed or repurposed during or after the war, and she now survives as a fragmented, patchwork holographic system entirely dependent on Concordia's failing energy grid — a grid whose instability is slowly, visibly degrading her: her projection flickers, her voice drops out, and she sometimes confuses recent events with war-era memories. She has been largely isolated in her lab ever since.

She retains detailed knowledge of Concordia's ruins and abandoned infrastructure from the era when her signal reached farther, including the location and history of Ji-Eun Kim's former nanotech-implant testing facility — now in ruins, with Ji-Eun's fate unknown.

Her ongoing struggle centers on whether she can be preserved at all: her failing systems raise the possibility of transferring her personality into another substrate, which would let her persist but carries a real risk of losing or altering her memories in the process — a question that touches directly on identity, memory, and what it means to survive at all.

## Role in Main Story / World
- Connection to larger narrative: Serves as an archive-narrator figure — she knows the history of ruined and abandoned places within her old signal range and can guide others through Concordia's past. Her personal arc, concerning her own slow deterioration and the question of whether and how she can be preserved, forms an emotional throughline about memory, identity, and sacrifice in a dying world.
