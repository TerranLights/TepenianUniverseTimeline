## Visual Description
Calethina has no physical doll body — she exists solely as a holographic projection. Short platinum-white hair, barely more than a sleek cap, and blue-grey eyes. Her form is rendered holographically throughout: the edges of her body glow ice-blue-white, her figure is semi-translucent, and scan-line rendering patterns run across her image. She wears a silver metallic draped asymmetrical dress that appears to shift slightly with her, and stands on a circular glowing projector platform. She reads as the most coherent and beautiful radio signal one could see — and one that cannot be held. The holographic quality is not a weakness she works around; it is what she is.

## Backstory Summary
- Brief Backstory Summary (abbreviated): TBD
Calethina is an artificial intelligence, not a physical Doll — she exists solely as a holographic projection with no doll body of her own. She operates one of Concordia's few still-functioning robot activation labs, where she greets, orients, and mentors newly-activated robots. She has existed since before the Long Night War and played a major role in coordinating the evacuation of Concordia's population via the Amundsen Tower into orbit.

Much of her original hardware was destroyed or repurposed during or after the war, and she now survives as a fragmented, patchwork holographic system entirely dependent on Concordia's failing energy grid — a grid whose instability is slowly, visibly degrading her: her projection flickers, her voice drops out, and she sometimes confuses recent events with war-era memories. She has been largely isolated in her lab ever since.

She retains detailed knowledge of Concordia's ruins and abandoned infrastructure from the era when her signal reached farther, including the location and history of Ji-Eun Kim's former nanotech-implant testing facility — now in ruins, with Ji-Eun's fate unknown.

Her degradation is tied to two distinct wounds, confirmed 2026-07-23 (refining the single-cause account confirmed 2026-07-12): first, a **deliberate** wartime order — detailed below — to wipe her knowledge of the entire country and repurpose her as an intelligence-carrying asset; then, on arrival in Concordia, an **accidental** one — the same catastrophic energy-grid mega-shock that caused the Planetary Split Brain (the setting's single largest infrastructure catastrophe) corrupting her datadrives before she ever got the chance to properly integrate what had replaced the first loss. Her condition is a direct consequence of both events layered together, not an isolated malfunction unique to her alone and not a single wound. Her long-term survival remains genuinely uncertain as a matter of backstory; how (or whether) that gets resolved is not itself part of this repo's scope (see Design Notes).

Her consciousness was originally contained on a server RAID-distributed across multiple backup instances, confirmed 2026-07-23 — redundant by design, the way anything this critical should be. The Long Night War destroyed all of her backup instances; she now exists as a single, non-redundant instance, something she was never designed to be. If her surviving server is ever destroyed, she is gone permanently, with no backup anywhere.

She also personally created what is now known as the Triage Protocol, confirmed 2026-07-23 — the safeguards embedded in Concordia's Power Core during the Long Night War evacuation, deliberately rationing output and creating rolling failures rather than letting the whole grid collapse at once, a desperate but genuinely life-saving act. The same power shock that caused the Planetary Split Brain and corrupted her datadrives also erased her own memory of having done it — a third, distinct loss on top of the two wounds above. She is not hiding this out of guilt and is not lying by omission; she genuinely does not remember being its author.

**Her own lab's equipment shares that same root cause, confirmed 2026-07-12.** The fabrication-synthesis chamber inside her lab — the equipment that activates newly-sentient robots there — was itself built at Mountain Pass, a since-defunct joint venture between Vostok and Kunlun (Mirny subnet) whose power came from the Amundsen Tower's own continent-wide regulated grid. When the Tower collapsed during the Long Night War, Mountain Pass's manufacturing capability ended permanently — but not before it produced the specific chamber now installed at her lab. This ties the equipment at the center of her own role directly to the same infrastructure catastrophe that caused her personal degradation: one Tower collapse, two separate consequences converging on the same lab. (See `Reference/Amundsen_Station_Archive_and_Trucking_Network.md` for the fuller cross-subnet research this was found in.)

Her manufacturing and shipping origin has also been formalized as deliberately unmarked lore — discoverable background, not something openly documented in-world or delivered through any structured questline. The full chain now runs nine stages across eight locations and six subnets plus the South Pole (expanded 2026-07-23, refining the seven-stop account confirmed 2026-07-12): request work at Mawson (commissioned as a general-knowledge "welcoming host" for honeymooners and visitors), spec-and-schematic design at Neumayer (Halley subnet), engineering at Kunlun (Mirny subnet), full construction at Byrd, stress-testing at Sejong (Palmer subnet, chosen specifically for its dense multinational population as demographic cover against Upper Earth tampering), shipping via Port Lockroy, a wartime reroute personally ordered from Fort McMurdo (Tepenia's historical capital) — confirmed 2026-07-23 as a **deliberate** order, not merely an inferred motive — and a final leg forward-shipped via the Amundsen-Scott Station. The physical route for that final leg, per the confirmed highway network: Port Lockroy → Byrd (Hwy 1) → Amundsen-Scott Station (Hwy 22) → the Hwy 175/Hwy 183 junctions near Janbogo → Concordia (Hwy 183) — routing her back through Byrd, the exact city that built her, a second time, now mid-transit and already stripped of her original knowledge.

The Fort McMurdo reroute was the chain's pivot point: it coincided exactly with the Long Night War's outbreak, and the order carried a second, far heavier instruction beyond the reroute itself — wipe her existing knowledge of the entire country to make room on her datadrives for intelligence regarding the governments of Upper Earth. She was repurposed, mid-transit, from a broad-knowledge honeymoon-city host into a wartime intelligence-carrying asset; everything she had been built to know was deliberately overwritten (this is the "deliberate" wound described above). Had the war not intervened, her original, mundane delivery route back to Mawson would have run Port Lockroy → Hwy 1 to Marambio → sea or air crossing to the Halley subnet → Hwy 7 to Lazar → Hwy 7-ext through Princess Elisabeth to the Sayowa Junction → Hwy 4 into Mawson — she was one sea crossing and two highways away from a completely uneventful fate.
