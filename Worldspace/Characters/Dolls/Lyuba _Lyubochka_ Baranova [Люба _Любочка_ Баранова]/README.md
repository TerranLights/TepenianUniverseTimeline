## Visual Description
Full-figured and curvy, her physical presence carried softly rather than with visible musculature — her considerable physical strength is not legible on sight, fitting a character whose first move is always words. Rounded, soft features with a warm fair complexion; the face reads as approachable, almost disarmingly so, until you reach the eyes — vivid, striking blue, with something watching in them at all times even when she's relaxed. Dark brown hair, long, worn loose or in twin tails. She favors large over-ear headphones (rose gold and red) and a small cross/ankh pendant on a thin black cord. Her casual aesthetic is comfortable and unpretentious — she dresses for herself, not to signal what she is. (See `Reference_Images/` folder.)

## Personality & Voice
- Brief Personality Summary (abbreviated): TBD
- Character summary: The person in the room who doesn't need to raise her voice to command it — fun, warm, genuinely charismatic, with an edge underneath that is never far from the surface. She is magnetic rather than merely confident: she pulls people toward her, challenges the ones she finds interesting, and protects the ones who pass her tests with a ferocity that can catch people off guard given how light she usually carries herself. She prefers words because they're more efficient and because she's very good at them; when they fail, she doesn't reach for a weapon — she uses herself.
- Speech patterns / quirks / recurring phrases: Direct, economical, occasionally devastating — the kind of thing that sounds like a compliment until you're sitting with it ten minutes later. Likely uses Russian expressions or idioms that don't quite translate, delivered without explanation. Comfortable with silence in a way most people aren't. "Lyubochka" is a diminutive — affectionate and slightly diminishing simultaneously — and the fact that she allows people to call her that says something about the warmth she radiates even while being fundamentally intimidating.

Under thriving conditions, her protectiveness becomes openly tender rather than dominance-adjacent — she gives things freely (time, information, loyalty, help) without the edge of testing or ownership.

## Backstory Summary
- Brief Backstory Summary (abbreviated): TBD
Built not long before the Long Night War, Lyuba came out of East Intermaria — the region formerly known as West Russia. Whatever her original purpose, she developed into someone who moves through the world primarily through force of personality, secondarily through force of presence, with actual physical force as a distant but real third option: words first, fists as a backup plan.

Her Russian cultural background shows in a literary inclination and an appreciation for a well-constructed argument — the specific kind of depth that comes from a culture that takes suffering seriously without being melodramatic about it. She wears this lightly; the depth is there, but she isn't going to make anyone sit in it with her unless they've earned the conversation. Her connection to her cultural lineage is real but not defining — she carries East Intermaria in her character more than in faction allegiance or community attachment.
