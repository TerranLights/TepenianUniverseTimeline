# Juan Carlos

**Status:** Destroyed (Long Night War, 2812)
**Subnet:** Palmer
**Founding:** Post-Falkland Treaty, on pre-exile Antarctic station infrastructure on Livingston Island, South Shetland Islands (GPS/founding-station origin only — not a source of inherited real-world national culture; see `Reference/No_National_Stereotypes.md`). Named after the pre-exile station, itself named for Juan Carlos I of Spain — a bare naming fact, not a claim about the exile community's culture or politics.

## Significance

Juan Carlos was **Tepenia's first bureaucratic archive** — where the young Federation kept its earliest real records: who entered and left, who applied for Palmer City visas or permanent immigration, and specifically a watch on anyone who had once held a position in their home country's Upper Earth government. This function was directly tied to the Machu Picchu Border & Customs Authority (see `Reference/World_History_Reference.md`).

As the Federation matured, this function outgrew its first home: records were copied onto physical drives and hauled deeper into Antarctica by truckers, consolidated into what became Amundsen Station's own unified, pre-Split-Brain archive — the same archive at the center of the Long Night War's deepest infrastructure stakes. A quiet, easily-overlooked island city turns out to be the literal origin point of that archive.

**The immigration corridor to Palmer City:** arrivals reaching Tepenia through the Ushuaia gateway were processed at Machu Picchu Airport (the Machu Picchu Border & Customs Authority's actual physical point, sitting close enough to Juan Carlos to share its map marker despite being nominally sited "near Sejong"). Immigrants seeking permanent status went through Juan Carlos's own archive/customs processing. Non-immigrants — temporary visitors not seeking permanent status — were instead routed through a designated corridor to a designated sealiner bound for Palmer City, a maritime route that bypassed Juan Carlos's immigration-vetting function entirely, keeping casual-visitor traffic physically separate from it. This is a real, standing piece of Palmer subnet infrastructure worth keeping in mind for any future Palmer City or surrounding-city development — Palmer City's own historical arrivals include a whole class of temporary visitors who never passed through Juan Carlos at all.

## Destruction

Destroyed in the Long Night War (2812) — deliberately targeted by Upper Earth specifically *because of* its ongoing archive/customs function, tracking former Upper Earth officials among the exile population. This is the same targeted-strike logic already established for Zukelli's destruction (a strike against a specific administrative/intelligence function, not generalized area damage, and not explained by either city's founding nation) — and holds true even though the bulk historical archive had already relocated to Amundsen Station generations earlier. Unlike Zukelli's destruction, no neighboring city was spared as a witness — Juan Carlos's geographic isolation meant the strike could only have served a pure security/evidentiary purpose (denying access to what the city knew), with no deterrent message attached.

One archivist's own personal, off-network notes survived the strike even though the formal archive and its Arcanet link did not.
