# The Amundsen Station Archive & Trucking Network — Research Findings (2026-07-12)

**What this is:** a full research pass across all 34 of Tepenia's city Megasheets (excluding Concordia, which has its own Ultra-Megasheet/district system), Concordia's own Ultra-Megasheet and 13 district files, the 5 Subnet Ultra-Megasheets, the Super-Ultra-Megasheet, and Amundsen Station's own Megasheet — hunting for two things: (1) any city connected to the physical trucking/data-hauling network that fed Amundsen Station's pre-Split-Brain archive, and (2) any connection anywhere in that material to Calethina. Conducted by six parallel research passes (one per subnet plus one for Concordia/Amundsen Station), each reading every assigned document in full, not just keyword-matching. This document preserves the full findings; see `Worldspace/Characters/Dolls/Calethina/README.md` and `Worldspace/Locations/Juan_Carlos.md` for the condensed, canon-integrated versions.

## Thread 1: The Physical Data-Hauling Network

### The origin: Juan Carlos (Palmer subnet)

Already fully documented in `Worldspace/Locations/Juan_Carlos.md` and `Reference/World_History_Reference.md`. Juan Carlos was Tepenia's first bureaucratic archive; its records were copied onto physical drives and hauled deeper into Antarctica by truckers, consolidated into what became Amundsen Station's own unified pre-Split-Brain archive.

The Palmer subnet's own README material does not elaborate further on the physical trucking route itself beyond what's already captured in the two files above — this was confirmed by a full re-read of Juan Carlos's, Sejong's, and the Palmer Subnet Ultra-Megasheet's own documents during this pass.

### Direct physical infrastructure links to Amundsen Station, found elsewhere

**Halley (Halley subnet) — the clearest direct physical link found anywhere in this search.** Halley is reached via "Hwy 59 (doubles as the Arcanet cable corridor south to Amundsen Station)." Its Full Extrapolation elaborates: "the physical road itself remains passable in sections, but its original dual purpose — highway and Arcanet cable corridor at once — has collapsed to just the first. With Amundsen Station destroyed, the highway's own southern terminus no longer functions as any kind of relay, meaning Hwy 59 today serves purely as a physical travel route with no remaining informational purpose." (`City_Megasheets/Halley_Subnet/Halley/README.md`)

**Byrd — a live, still-functioning truck route running directly through the Amundsen Station ruins.** Byrd's entire civic identity is built around trucking and dispatch: "keeping half the country running through a two-directional truck route it can no longer reach by air... Hwy 1 north toward the Peninsula and, beyond it, South America; Hwy 22 east across the entire continent to the Zhongshan/Soyuz/Shirayuki tri-junction, passing directly through the Amundsen Station ruins along the way." Byrd hosts "a massive trucking depot" and "the Dispatch Office (import/export & dispatch) — controls the trucking depot and everything that physically enters or leaves Byrd via Hwy 1 and Hwy 22." Its own Cross-Reference Synthesis states plainly: "for Byrd's eastern freight route to actually be functioning today, some passage through or around Amundsen Station's ruins has to exist — either a maintained bypass around the debris field, or a cleared channel through it, kept open specifically because too many cities' supply chains depend on it to simply abandon." Byrd also manufactures the trucks themselves — the "Rastra"/Arrastradora heavy-hauler lineage. (`City_Megasheets/Byrd_Subnet/Byrd/README.md`)

**Belgrano (Halley subnet) — origin of the truck itself.** "Belgrano built Tepenia's first native overland vehicles, 'las Arrastradoras' (worn down over generations into the international 'Rastra'), and sent an all-robot crew that discovered the buried ruins of Byrd Station — using maps drawn from its own archives, with material support shipped in from Palmer City and Rothera." (`City_Megasheets/Halley_Subnet/Belgrano/README.md`)

**Neumayer (Halley subnet) — designed the Tower itself, and is proposed as a secondary archive.** "Neumayer's single most significant legacy is the schematics, engineering design, and structural planning behind the Amundsen Tower itself." Its Cross-Reference Synthesis explicitly proposes it as "a genuinely plausible second significant archive location in Tepenia — not as complete or centrally important as Amundsen Station's own pre-Split-Brain archive, but a real, earned reason the city might hold valuable corroborating history," tied to the still-open question "whether Neumayer's own rigorous record-keeping habits extend to material relevant to the Planetary Split Brain crisis." (`City_Megasheets/Halley_Subnet/Neumayer/README.md`, echoed in the Halley Subnet Ultra-Megasheet)

**Fort McMurdo (Janbogo/Ross subnet) — built the Tower's supporting infrastructure; a plausible motive for its unexplained reroute in Calethina's shipping chain.** "Building the Tower — a national infrastructure project on a scale nothing else in Tepenian history matched — would have made the nation's dependency on Fort McMurdo's industrial output completely undeniable," and Fort McMurdo's own capital-status formalization is explicitly proposed as tied to the Tower's construction. Given that Fort McMurdo already personally ordered an otherwise-unexplained reroute in Calethina's own construction/shipping chain (see her character file), this gives a plausible in-world reason: a city whose civic identity and capital legitimacy are tied to supplying Tower infrastructure would have unusual interest in, or leverage over, anything else routed through or near it. (`City_Megasheets/Janbogo_Subnet/Fort_McMurdo/README.md`)

**Kunlun (Mirny subnet) — physical highway link plus a direct observational tie to the Tower's fall.** "A real physical highway link exists via Hwy 37." Its Full Extrapolation proposes "Kunlun's observatory is also one of the only facilities in Tepenia positioned to have tracked orbital debris from the Amundsen Tower's own collapse — meaning... a direct, still-untapped record of exactly what happened to Tepenia's own space elevator when it came down." (`City_Megasheets/Mirny_Subnet/Kunlun/README.md`)

**Zhongshan (Mirny subnet) — hosts its own pre-Split-Brain archive.** Proposed landmark: "The Long Record — the city's primary archive, custodian of the most complete pre-Split-Brain history in the Mirny subnet." No detail given on how records got there or a link to Amundsen Station specifically. (`City_Megasheets/Mirny_Subnet/Zhongshan/README.md`)

### General trucking/freight infrastructure (not Amundsen-specific)

- **Sanay** (Halley subnet): "Truckyards and warehouses occupy the lower city; the high rocky clifftops hold the major trucking/shipping depots and the import-export business district," co-located with the Halley subnet's actual Arcanet relay nexus. Routes freight two ways: "trucked directly to nearby cities via Hwy 7, or trucked onward to Troll."
- **Troll** (Halley subnet): freight/aviation chokepoint — "raw materials, goods, and supplies imported from Africa land at Sanay or Belgrano, get trucked overland via Hwy 7, and fly out across the whole country via Troll Airfield."
- **Soyuz** (Mirny subnet): "raw materials trucked in from Davis, worked into finished goods" — local industrial trucking, plus the Mountain Pass connection (see Thread 2 below).
- **Scott** (Janbogo/Ross subnet): a local "trucking facility" for volcanic material from Mount Erebus, delivered across McMurdo Sound — unrelated to the archive network.
- **Rothera** (Palmer subnet): internal Hwy 1 distribution of highway-maintenance vehicles and construction materials to other Palmer subnet cities — not connected to Amundsen Station.

### Confirmed no connection

Cape Adare, Denison, Dumont d'Urville, Janbogo, Zukelli (Janbogo/Ross subnet); Dome Fuji, Mawson (Mawson subnet); Casey, Davis, Mirny, Shirayuki, Vostok (Mirny subnet); Esperanza, Marambio, Palmer City, Port Lockroy, Sejong, Signy (Palmer subnet); Abowasa, Lazar, Princess Elisabeth (Halley subnet) — none of these cities' own Megasheets contain any reference to trucking, hauling, physical drives, Amundsen Station, or Hwy 22/175 specifically. Concordia's own Ultra-Megasheet, all 13 district Mega-Inits, the Amundsen Station Megasheet itself, and the Super-Ultra-Megasheet also contain **no** trucking/hauling language at all — the physical route mechanics live entirely in the city-tier documents listed above, not in any higher-level synthesis document.

## Thread 2: Calethina

### The headline finding — her lab's chamber and the Amundsen Tower's collapse are directly linked

The fabrication-synthesis chamber inside Calethina's own lab — the equipment that activates newly-sentient robots there — was manufactured at **Mountain Pass**, a since-defunct joint venture between **Vostok and Kunlun** (both Mirny subnet), located on Hwy 37. Mountain Pass's own power came from **residual overflow off the Amundsen Tower's continent-wide regulated grid** — it was too remote to support its own dedicated power infrastructure. When the Tower collapsed during the Long Night War, that supply ended permanently, and Mountain Pass's manufacturing capability died with it — but not before it had produced the specific chamber now installed at Calethina's lab.

This fact is stated independently, and almost identically, in two separate documents:

- **Mirny Subnet Ultra-Megasheet:** "one of those chambers, built at Mountain Pass before the war, is confirmed as the specific chamber in Calethina's own lab that built the player character" — explicitly called "the single most consequential fact this whole Ultra-Megasheet series has produced to date," flagged as "worth keeping close at hand for any future work touching the player character's own backstory, Calethina's lab, or the Tower's own central role in the game's lore." (`City_Megasheets/Mirny_Subnet/Mirny_Subnet_Ultra_Megasheet/README.md`)
- **Super-Ultra-Megasheet, Cross-Regional Throughway 1:** traces the full "Cradle" chamber-manufacturing network — Neumayer's original design schematic → Byrd and Soyuz's present-day active manufacturing → Denison's and Mountain Pass's historical, now-defunct manufacturing — and states: "Mountain Pass depended on residual power overflow from the Amundsen Tower's own continent-wide regulated grid... the Tower's destruction... ended Mountain Pass's manufacturing capability permanently... but not before Mountain Pass produced the specific chamber, installed at Calethina's own lab in Concordia, that created the player character." (`Worldspace/Locations-and-Levels/Super_Ultra_Megasheet/README.md`)
- **Soyuz's own README** carries the same fact at city level: "Chambers built there before the war still function wherever they shipped to (including, per a quiet non-quest discoverable detail, the specific chamber in Calethina's own lab that built the player character); no new ones have been made there since the war." (`City_Megasheets/Mirny_Subnet/Soyuz/README.md`)

Note: Kunlun's own README, despite being confirmed as one half of the Mountain Pass joint venture, does not itself state the Calethina connection — that link only surfaces in Soyuz's file and the two Ultra-Megasheet-tier synthesis documents above. This repo's own framing (in Calethina's character file) generalizes "created the player character" to "activates newly-sentient robots there," since "player character" is CRPG-exclusive framing outside this repo's scope.

### Her lab's precise location

Two independent sources place her lab at a **three-district corner — Cancer, Taurus, and Capricorn** — not simply a Cancer/Capricorn border as this repo previously stated:

- **Concordia Ultra-Megasheet:** "Calethina's Lab — the game's own primary activation site for newly sentient robots, physically located at the Cancer/Taurus/Capricorn corner — gives Concordia a genuine, mechanical answer to [who gets built or activated in Concordia versus who arrives as a refugee]... Robots currently active in Concordia are disproportionately likely to have been built and activated there directly, simply because the city hosts the activation infrastructure." (`Concordia_Ultra_Megasheet/README.md`)
- **Hub district's own Mega-Init:** "Calethina's Engineering & Activation Lab, though physically sited at Cancer's outer edge, shares the same neutral-jurisdiction founding logic [as the Hub]." Hub residents include "researchers attached to Calethina's Lab" and "activation and integration specialists from Calethina's Lab." Also, under a Panama Canal Zone real-world-inspiration entry: "echoed directly in Calethina's Lab's own siting at a three-district corner rather than inside any single district." (`District_Megasheets/13_Hub/Hub_Mega_Init.md`)

Neither Cancer's nor Capricorn's own dedicated Megasheet documents (Mega-Init, Full Extrapolation, and Cross-Reference Synthesis — all three checked directly, and via a targeted repo-wide grep) contain any mention of Calethina at all. Her lab's presence is only carried in the Concordia Ultra-Megasheet and the Hub district's own file — not in either of the two districts it's supposedly sited at the corner of.

### Open canvas — what's ours to develop together

Across all 34 city Megasheets, all 13 Concordia district files, the Concordia Ultra-Megasheet, the Amundsen Station Megasheet, and the Super-Ultra-Megasheet, none of the following appear anywhere outside Calethina's own dedicated character file — meaning none of it is locked in anywhere else, and all of it is genuinely open to develop from scratch here:
- Her holographic, bodiless nature
- Her role in the Amundsen Tower evacuation
- Her degradation being tied to the Planetary Split Brain (the Mountain Pass/chamber link above is a *supply-chain* connection to the Tower's collapse, not a personal/narrative one — the two are established independently of each other)
- Ji-Eun Kim, beyond a single bare namedrop in Zhongshan's "already named" notable-figures list, with no further explanation given there

### Sejong and Port Lockroy

Two cities named as confirmed stops in Calethina's own construction/shipping chain — **Sejong** (stress-testing) and **Port Lockroy** (shipping route) — don't say anything about that connection in their own city Megasheets. That's expected rather than a problem to fix: her chain lives in her own character file, and this is simply the raw material for whatever gets developed about her and these two cities going forward.
