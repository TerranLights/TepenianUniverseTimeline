# The Falkland Treaty — Structural Scaffold

**Status:** structure only, no final prose drafted yet. See `Real_World_Influences.md` in this folder for the real-world treaties behind each choice below, and `Worldspace/World_History_Reference.md` for the established canon this scaffold must remain consistent with.

**Format:** one single treaty document, constitution-style — a Preamble followed by Titles containing nested Articles, not a flat list like the real Antarctic Treaty. A separate Tepenian Constitution may exist as a future companion document; this treaty is not that document, though it does the work of founding the country.

**Genre, corrected 2026-07-08:** this is a victor's settlement imposed on a defeated, exiled population — not a cooperative agreement among equals. Signed by the future-era equivalent of the UN (for Upper Earth) against named, high-ranking robot leaders negotiating on behalf of their people, from a position of certain defeat. Voice throughout should balance one-sided victor's language with a neutral, negotiated-seeming register — per the developer's own framing, "negotiating with the robots to all choose between being exiled or being essentially destroyed." Named for its actual signing location, Falkland Island.

**Scope, confirmed 2026-07-08:** the treaty founds Tepenia as a sovereign entity and sets the terms of exile. It does **not** specify what kind of government the exiled population sets up — Tepenia has its own real historical national capital (Fort McMurdo, through the Long Night War) and its own government, whose internal structure is simply undecided so far, deliberately deferred to a separate future Tepenian Constitution document. The Falkland Treaty stays out of that question entirely, the same way Versailles didn't dictate Germany's own internal constitution.

---

## Preamble

Written in the New Echota register: flat, procedural, and — critically — narrated so that the treaty *appears* to be a genuine negotiation with a legitimately-mandated robot leadership, regardless of how clean that mandate actually was. States the war's end plainly (Versailles-style factual recitation, no editorializing) without stating outright that extermination was the alternative on the table; that reality should be visible only to a reader who already knows the history, the same way New Echota's preamble never admits the "choice" it describes was coerced.

**Confirmed robot signatory, 2026-07-09 (previously tentative as of 2026-07-08): Maggie Aarden** — see `Worldspace/Characters/Dolls/Still-Present_-_In-Game/Maggie Aarden/README.md`. **Confirmed circumstances, corrected 2026-07-11:** she did not step forward — she was "invited" to "volunteer" by **Upper Earth's own Assembly Powers**, who had already settled on her before she was ever consulted, the same coercion-dressed-as-choice the preamble's own New Echota register is built to obscure. Her established character sharpens the preamble's whole tonal problem rather than complicating it: an SP 5w6 ("The Castle") who never wanted to lead, forced into it by her own w6 loyalty rather than ambition, whose prior solution to the exact same problem — rising anti-robot sentiment in Midwestland and the Colorado Republic — was already "a third option": not fighting, not submitting, just quietly resettling into separate parallel communities. The Falkland Treaty's exile is that same instinct taken to its final, forced, national conclusion — and her own signing of it was extracted the exact same way. A leader like that would negotiate exile pragmatically and minimally, not defiantly — which is exactly the register the New Echota model calls for, and gives the preamble's manufactured-legitimacy narration a real psychological engine instead of just a structural device.

**Open questions for this section:** the exact name of Upper Earth's UN-equivalent signatory body (currently unnamed anywhere in canon); whether a Westphalia-style mutual-amnesty ("perpetual oblivion") clause belongs here, contingent on whether the founding nations carried real pre-war conflict among themselves.

## Title I — Founding the Federation of Tepenia

Leads the treaty's substantive content, per the Versailles precedent of nesting a new institution's charter as Part I of a punitive settlement. Formally establishes the Federation of Tepenia as a sovereign political entity in Antarctica — the exiled population's own country, not an administered territory of Upper Earth. Confirms the Federation's founding date (June 21, 2564, already established canon) and that its sovereignty is total, full stop — deliberately not specifying *how* that sovereignty gets exercised internally, which is Constitution territory.

This is also where the treaty does its most New-Echota-like work: presenting national self-governance as something granted/secured for the robots and their allies, in the same register New Echota grants the Cherokee "the right by their national councils to make and carry into effect all such laws as they may deem necessary" — without the treaty itself ever specifying what those national councils actually look like.

## Title II — Territorial & Population Provisions

**Article II.1 — The Sovereignty Freeze.** Adapted from the Antarctic Treaty's Article IV: no Upper Earth signatory nation's prior Antarctic territorial claim is strengthened, renounced, or newly assertable by anything done under this treaty. This is about Upper Earth's own real, overlapping claimant nations (UK/Argentina/Chile-style disputes) getting folded into one exile settlement without first resolving whose claim was valid — **not** about Tepenia's own internal government, which the treaty leaves entirely alone (see Scope, above).

**Article II.2 — The Exile Provision.** The core, darkest clause: exile applies to robots as a class, and to their human allies, not to named individuals — consistent with established canon (Ji-Eun Kim "was not singled out as dangerous... simply caught in a clause that applied to her entire category of existence"). Should be stated with Versailles-style concrete specificity rather than vague deferral, while the New Echota-style diplomatic register keeps the surrounding language from reading as openly punitive.

**Article II.3 — Population Allocation Mechanism.** The general mechanism (of which the later Jeju-do-administered Bharati/Shirayuki allocation was one instance, centuries downstream) — how founding populations get assigned to real Antarctic station sites. Structured loosely on the Lateran Treaty's clean, few-article approach to vesting sovereignty over a bounded territory, adapted for population rather than land.

**Article II.4 — Path to Full Civic Independence.** A Trusteeship-System-style built-in trajectory from temporary/administered status toward full self-governance at the *city* level — the real-world precedent for the Tri-Cities' already-established arc (legal separation → de facto amalgamation → full legal unification). This is about individual founding cities' own civic maturation, not the national government question, which stays out of this treaty entirely.

## Title III — Regional Governance Framework

**Article III.1 — Regional Arrangements.** Adapted directly from UN Charter Chapter VIII: local disputes handled by regional bodies before escalating to any central authority. This is the treaty-level justification the subnet system has never had — Tepenia's six subnets (Halley, Palmer, Janbogo/Ross, Mirny, Mawson, Byrd) function as semi-autonomous governance units *because the founding treaty structured them that way*, not just as an emergent map convention. This stops short of specifying Tepenia's own national government — it only establishes that sub-national regional bodies have standing under the treaty, leaving what sits above them for the Constitution to define.

**Article III.2 — Jeju-do Note (clarification, not treaty content).** Confirmed 2026-07-08: Jeju-do's own Court is *not* this treaty's authoring body — it's a much older, separate Korea/Japan/Sinian-Federation regional institution, established a generation or two after 2083, centuries before the Falkland Treaty. Whatever body Upper Earth's side actually used to negotiate remains its own open question, not to be conflated with Jeju-do. This article is a scaffold-note, not necessarily treaty prose — flagged here so it isn't lost before drafting.

## Title IV — Rights, Restrictions & Peaceful Use

The treaty's most brutal content, deliberately placed after the institution-founding sections rather than before them (Versailles-style ordering: found the entity, then impose the terms).

**Article IV.1 — Personhood Revocation.** States plainly that the 2318 Jeju-do ruling's 246 years of established robot legal personhood is stripped for any robot remaining on or returning to Upper Earth soil, without extending that revocation to robots within Tepenia's own sovereign territory (a carve-out Tepenia's government would have had to fight for, or that Upper Earth conceded as the price of a clean exile rather than tracking every individual).

**Article IV.2 — Peaceful Use & Demilitarization.** Not an absolute ban like the Antarctic Treaty's — Locarno-style named exceptions instead (self-defense, collective enforcement action, action against a first-mover aggressor). This is deliberately the clause with a built-in loophole: whichever exception gets exploited centuries later is the actual mechanical seed of the Long Night War.

---

## Title V — Scientific, Economic & Energy Cooperation

**Article V.1 — Nuclear Prohibition.** Inherited from the Antarctic Treaty's Article V. Retroactively explains why Tepenia's civilization runs on the Amundsen Tower's grid-regulation model instead of nuclear power — not a technological gap, a treaty-mandated one from the founding.

**Article V.2 — Compensation Mechanism.** Modeled on the Lateran Treaty's Financial Convention: the stronger party (Upper Earth) provides some form of resource or technology transfer to the newly-founded Federation as part of final settlement — inadequate to the loss, but present, giving the exile a face-saving quality it wouldn't have as pure banishment. Open question: what this compensation actually consists of.

**Article V.3 — General Cooperation.** Lighter-touch provisions on trade/contact between Tepenia and Upper Earth going forward, precedent for whatever the game's established Upper Earth shipping/trade lore (South Africa, New Zealand, Australia routes) assumes was always legally permitted.

---

## Title VI — Inspection & Transparency

Written with real force — per the Antarctic Treaty's Article VII, unconditional mutual inspection rights — specifically so that its eventual violation (someone building something in secret, centuries later) reads as the treaty's actual failure point, not just background to "a war happened."

---

## Title VII — Amendment, Review & Withdrawal

**Article VII.1 — Dispute Resolution Ladder.** Locarno-style tiered escalation: judicial decision for rights questions, conciliation for everything else, central authority only if conciliation fails, collective response only after a formal finding.

**Article VII.2 — Scheduled Review.** A built-in checkpoint across the ~248-year span between the Falkland Treaty and the Long Night War — giving the Second Interwar Period's long timeline at least one moment where the whole system could plausibly have been revisited. Whether it actually was is a separate, open historical question.

**Article VII.3 — Withdrawal Clause.** The single most dangerous mechanism in this scaffold: a Partial-Test-Ban-style "supreme interest" withdrawal right, letting any signatory legally exit if it judges the treaty's continuation to threaten its own vital interests. This is the candidate mechanical origin of the Long Night War — not an external villain, not a hidden betrayal, just a founding nation legally invoking a clause the treaty always guaranteed it, and that legitimate act cascading into catastrophe.

---

## Worth Your Attention

Title II.2 (the exile provision) and Title VII.3 (the withdrawal clause) are the two places where this document is doing the most quietly devastating work — one because it's the treaty's actual founding cruelty, the other because it's the seed of the war that ends everything else this document builds. Both should get drafted with the most care, and both benefit most from the New Echota register: matter-of-fact, procedurally legitimate, never announcing what it's actually doing. Also worth noting: this scaffold now cleanly separates "what the Falkland Treaty governs" (exile terms, sovereignty, Upper-Earth-facing provisions) from "what the Tepenian Constitution will govern" (actual internal government structure) — a boundary that didn't exist in the first draft of this scaffold and needed a developer correction to fix.
