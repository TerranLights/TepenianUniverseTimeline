# The Falkland Treaty

**Status:** first full draft, 2026-07-08. Not locked canon. Drafted directly from `Scaffold.md` and `Real_World_Influences.md` in this folder — see both for the reasoning behind every choice below. Signed at the city of Stanley, Falkland Islands, June 21, 2564.

**Confirmed, 2026-07-08:** the United Assembly of Nations is a near-universal body — membership is the default, and the meaningful list is the short one of who is *not* a member: a handful of small nations with negligible international presence and no robot populations of their own (real-world examples given by the developer: Guam, Papua New Guinea, St. Kitts & Nevis, Tuvalu, Seychelles, Fiji, and similar). This treaty's preamble reflects that — it names the Assembly's confirmed member-nations only as illustrative examples, not as an exhaustive list, since the Assembly's actual membership is effectively "everyone but a short exclusion list."

**Proposed, not yet confirmed:** the exact composition of the Assembly's negotiating delegation at Stanley. **Maggie Aarden's role as the robot signatory is confirmed, 2026-07-09; the mechanism corrected 2026-07-11** — see her `README.md` in `Worldspace/Characters/Dolls/Still-Present_-_In-Game/Maggie Aarden/` (InnerTepeniaGDD). The treaty's own line 17 below ("called upon... to speak and act on their behalf") is the New Echota register doing its work — in-universe, it's meant to read as a genuine mandate; the confirmed reality is that it was the **Assembly Powers themselves** who "invited" her to "volunteer," having already settled on her — a credible, war-recognized robot leader — as the signature that would make the exile look consented-to rather than simply imposed on the defeated.

## THE FALKLAND TREATY

*Concluded at the city of Stanley, Falkland Islands, the twenty-first day of June, in the year 2564*

The United Assembly of Nations, comprising in its membership the governments of Upper Earth in near entirety — among them Unified Korea, the Sinian Federation, the Sovereign Republic of Hawaii, Midwestland, the Colorado Republic, the People's Democratic Republic of Cascadia, the Appalachia Republic, the Prairie Federation, the Republic of Sonora, the Republic of Alaska, and the People's Democratic Republic of New England — these Powers, together with the whole of the Assembly's membership, being described in the present Treaty as the Assembly Powers, of the one part;

And Maggie Aarden, called upon by the community of robots and their human allies to speak and act on their behalf, and by them fully empowered to conclude the present Treaty, of the other part;

Recognizing that the state of hostilities known as the War of Upper Earth has continued between them since the year 2563, to the great cost of all parties and to the disruption of the ordinary life of every nation named above;

Desiring to bring that state of hostilities to a final and durable close, and to arrive at arrangements consistent with the dignity, the security, and the future prospects of all peoples party to this Treaty;

Acknowledging that the robot population, together with those humans who stood beside them, have sought and been granted a homeland of their own, separate from the territories of the Assembly Powers, in which to build and govern their own society according to their own judgment, free from the conditions that gave rise to the present conflict;

Have agreed as follows:

---

### TITLE I — FOUNDING THE FEDERATION OF TEPENIA

**Article I.1.** There is established, upon the continent of Antarctica, a sovereign nation to be known as the Federation of Tepenia, founded upon the effective date of this Treaty and continuing without term thereafter.

**Article I.2.** The Federation of Tepenia is recognized by the Assembly Powers as fully sovereign within the territory described in Article II.3 below. No Assembly Power shall assert jurisdiction, administrative authority, or right of governance over the Federation of Tepenia or any person lawfully resident within it, save as expressly reserved elsewhere in this Treaty.

**Article I.3.** The internal form of government, the offices, and the laws by which the Federation of Tepenia governs itself are matters for its own people to determine, and no provision of this Treaty shall be read to require, forbid, or presume any particular form thereof. This Treaty settles the terms upon which the Federation of Tepenia comes to exist; it does not settle how the Federation of Tepenia chooses to govern itself once it does.

**Article I.4.** The right of the Federation of Tepenia's own communities and settlements to organize their own local councils, to make and carry into effect such laws as they deem necessary for their own welfare and protection, and to conduct their own affairs among themselves, is hereby secured, subject always to the provisions of Title II of this Treaty.

---

### TITLE II — TERRITORIAL & POPULATION PROVISIONS

**Article II.1 — Sovereignty Freeze.** Nothing in the present Treaty shall be interpreted as a renunciation, by any Assembly Power, of any territorial claim it had previously asserted with respect to Antarctica, nor as a recognition or denial of the validity of any such claim, nor shall it be interpreted as creating, strengthening, or diminishing any such claim. No Assembly Power shall assert a new claim, or enlarge an existing one, by virtue of any act done under this Treaty. The question of which Power held the superior claim to any part of Antarctica prior to this Treaty is not settled by this Treaty, and is not to be regarded as requiring settlement before this Treaty may take effect.

**Article II.2 — The Exile Provision.**
1. Every robot presently residing within the territory of any Assembly Power, and every robot who may hereafter come into being within such territory, shall depart that territory and take up residence within the Federation of Tepenia, in accordance with such schedule and such means of conveyance as the Assembly Powers shall provide.
2. This provision applies to robots as a class. No individual designation, accusation, or finding of wrongdoing is required, sought, or relevant to its application; nor shall the absence of any such finding be grounds for exception.
3. Any human person who has, prior to the date of this Treaty, taken up residence among robot communities, married or partnered with a robot, borne or raised children in a robot household, or otherwise established their life among the robot population, may elect to accompany that population to the Federation of Tepenia, and shall not be prevented from doing so by any Assembly Power.
4. No human person shall be compelled to depart under this Article who does not so elect.

**Article II.3 — Population Allocation Mechanism.**
1. The territory of the Federation of Tepenia comprises the continent of Antarctica in its entirety, together with such surrounding waters as are necessary to its settlements' maritime access.
2. The population departing under Article II.2 shall be settled at such existing scientific and research installations as are presently found upon that continent, each population being assigned to a station according to arrangements to be concluded between the Assembly Powers and the government of the Federation of Tepenia in the years following this Treaty's signing.
3. Nothing in this Article requires that a population assigned to a given station share the national origin of the Power that formerly maintained it; the purpose of assignment is the orderly and humane distribution of the exiled population across habitable ground, not the perpetuation of any prior national arrangement.

**Article II.4 — Path to Full Civic Independence.** Any settlement founded under Article II.3, once established, may in time and by its own governance progress from its founding administration toward full and complete self-governing status, at whatever pace and by whatever internal process its own population determines, without requiring further leave of the Assembly Powers to do so.

---

### TITLE III — REGIONAL GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK

**Article III.1 — Regional Arrangements.** The settlements established under Title II may, at their own initiative, associate into regional groupings for the resolution of local questions, the coordination of shared infrastructure, and the maintenance of order among themselves, before any such question is referred to the government of the Federation of Tepenia as a whole. Nothing in this Article shall be read to define what authority sits above such regional groupings; that is a matter for the Federation's own internal arrangement.

---

### TITLE IV — RIGHTS, RESTRICTIONS & PEACEFUL USE

**Article IV.1 — Status of Robot Persons.**
1. The legal personhood of robots, as recognized within the jurisdiction of the Assembly Powers since the ruling of the International Court of Diplomacy at Jeju-do of the 27th day of April, 2318, is, as to the territory of the Assembly Powers, withdrawn as of the effective date of this Treaty. No robot shall thereafter hold, within Assembly Power territory, the rights, protections, or standing of a legal person.
2. This withdrawal has no application within the territory of the Federation of Tepenia, where the legal standing of robots shall be a matter for that Federation's own law to determine, free of any obligation arising from this Article.
3. A robot who has departed Assembly Power territory in accordance with Article II.2 and does not thereafter return to it is not affected by paragraph 1 of this Article.

**Article IV.2 — Status of Human Persons Electing Exile.**
1. No provision of this Treaty shall be read to deprive any human person of legal personhood by reason of that person's own nature, nor shall any human person be subject to Article IV.1.
2. A human person who elects to accompany the robot population to the Federation of Tepenia under Article II.2.3 shall, by that election, be deemed under the law of each Assembly Power to have willingly and permanently relinquished their legal personhood within Assembly Power territory, and shall not thereafter be recognized, within any such territory, as a person in law.
3. No human person who has made the election described in paragraph 2 may thereafter return to the territory of any Assembly Power; no Assembly Power is obliged to recognize, admit, or extend any right to such a person who attempts to do so.
4. The forfeiture described in this Article is not a penalty imposed by any Assembly Power, but a consequence, in the law of each Assembly Power, of the human person's own free choice to depart.

**Article IV.3 — Peaceful Use and the Prohibition of Renewed Hostility.**
1. Neither the Assembly Powers nor the Federation of Tepenia shall resort to armed force against the other, save in the following cases: the exercise of legitimate self-defense against an armed attack already underway; collective action taken in concert by a majority of the Assembly Powers together with the government of the Federation of Tepenia, directed against a party found to have violated this Article; or action directed against whichever party is found to have been the first to resort to force.
2. Nothing in this Article prevents either party from maintaining such forces as it judges necessary for its own internal order and defense, provided that no such force is used in a manner inconsistent with paragraph 1.

---

### TITLE V — SCIENTIFIC, ECONOMIC & ENERGY COOPERATION

**Article V.1 — Prohibition of Nuclear Devices.** No nuclear explosive device of any kind shall be constructed, tested, or detonated within the territory of the Federation of Tepenia, nor shall any radioactive waste material be disposed of therein. This prohibition binds the Federation of Tepenia and its population without exception and without term.

**Article V.2 — Settlement Provision.** In recognition of the scale of the relocation required under Title II, the Assembly Powers shall provide to the government of the Federation of Tepenia, in the first ten years following this Treaty's signing, such material assistance — in transport, in raw material, and in technical instrument — as shall be specified in a schedule to be agreed between the Parties. No sum or quantity so provided shall be construed as satisfying any further obligation of the Assembly Powers, nor as a full accounting for what has been lost by the population under Title II.

**Article V.3 — General Cooperation.** The Assembly Powers and the Federation of Tepenia may, at any time following this Treaty's signing, conclude such further agreements as they find mutually useful concerning trade, the exchange of goods, and lawful passage between their respective territories, and nothing in this Treaty shall be read to forbid such agreements or to require that they take any particular form.

---

### TITLE VI — INSPECTION & TRANSPARENCY

**Article VI.1.** Each Party to this Treaty shall have the right, at any time, to designate observers who shall have complete and unrestricted access to any installation, settlement, vessel, or aircraft of the other Party, for the purpose of confirming that the provisions of this Treaty, and in particular of Title IV and Title V, are being observed.

**Article VI.2.** Each Party shall give advance notice to the other of any expedition, installation, or activity of a kind described in Article VI.1, sufficient to permit the exercise of the inspection right described therein.

**Article VI.3.** Neither Party shall obstruct, delay, or interfere with an inspection properly conducted under this Title.

---

### TITLE VII — AMENDMENT, REVIEW & WITHDRAWAL

**Article VII.1 — Resolution of Disputes.** Any dispute arising between the Parties as to the interpretation or application of this Treaty shall first be submitted to judicial determination, where the dispute concerns a question of right; all other disputes shall first be submitted to a joint conciliation commission composed of representatives of both Parties. Should conciliation fail, either Party may refer the matter to the full body of the United Assembly of Nations, whose finding shall be communicated to both Parties before any further action is taken.

**Article VII.2 — Periodic Review.** Not later than thirty years from the date of this Treaty's entry into force, and at intervals of not less than thirty years thereafter, either Party may call for a conference of both Parties to review the operation of this Treaty and to consider such amendments as changed circumstances may warrant. Neither Party is obliged to propose or accept any amendment considered at such a conference.

**Article VII.3 — Withdrawal.** Either Party may withdraw from this Treaty, or from any part of it, upon a determination by that Party that the continuation of this Treaty, or of the part in question, has come to threaten its own vital interests. Notice of such withdrawal shall be given to the other Party not less than three months before it takes effect.

---

### CLOSING PROVISIONS

**Article VIII.1.** This Treaty shall enter into force upon signature by both Parties, without further requirement of ratification.

**Article VIII.2.** This Treaty is done in a single original, to be deposited in the archives of the United Assembly of Nations, which shall provide a certified copy to the government of the Federation of Tepenia upon its formation.

*In witness whereof, the undersigned, being duly empowered to do so, have signed the present Treaty.*

**For the United Assembly of Nations and its member states:**
*[Assembly delegation — names not yet established]*

**For the robot population and their human allies:**
*Maggie Aarden* — *(confirmed 2026-07-09; see character file for the circumstances of her signing)*

## Notes on this draft

- **Deliberately left vague/unnamed:** the exact composition of the Assembly delegation (individual diplomat names, in the Versailles style, once the developer wants that level of detail); the exact figures/materials in Article V.2's settlement schedule; whether a Westphalia-style mutual amnesty clause belongs in the preamble (left out of this draft — the preamble as written doesn't reference any prior conflict among the Assembly Powers themselves, only the war against the robot population, so an amnesty clause didn't have an obvious place to sit; flag if that's wrong).
- **The preamble's central move:** it never states that extermination was the alternative to exile. It frames the whole treaty as something "sought and been granted," in the New Echota register — a reader who knows the history reads the gap; the document itself never admits it.
- **Article II.2.2** is the treaty's single most exposed line — stating plainly, without euphemism, that the exile is class-based and requires no individual finding. Left deliberately blunt rather than softened, per the scaffold's own note that this clause needed the most care and the least evasion.
- **Article IV.1.3** is new since the scaffold — a small clarifying provision making explicit that a robot who leaves and stays gone keeps their Tepenian standing, closing an ambiguity the scaffold's Article IV.1 note didn't fully resolve.
- **Article VII.3** is written exactly as dangerous as intended — no extra conditions, no committee approval required, just a unilateral "vital interests" judgment and a three-month clock. This is the clause meant to eventually break the whole treaty from the inside.
- **Article IV.2, added 2026-07-08:** humans are never stripped of personhood for being human — only for the specific, voluntary act of emigrating with the robot population. The legal framing (paragraph 4 especially) is deliberately voluntarist: Upper Earth's law characterizes this as the human's own free choice and its natural consequence, not a punishment imposed on them — the same New-Echota-style rhetorical move used throughout this draft, applied here to human exiles specifically. This also means human exiles are barred from return exactly as robots effectively are, even though the legal mechanism (forfeiture-by-choice vs. categorical revocation) is deliberately different for each.
- **Signatory list expanded 2026-07-08**, confirmed against an existing developer reference map (`Reference/Images/Maps/North America with tentative labels.jpg`) showing the full post-2083 fragmentation of the former United States. Now includes: Unified Korea, the Sinian Federation, the Sovereign Republic of Hawaii, Midwestland, the Colorado Republic, the People's Democratic Republic of Cascadia, the Appalachia Republic, the Prairie Federation, the Republic of Sonora, the Republic of Alaska, and the People's Democratic Republic of New England — eight named successor-states to the former US alone. **Deliberately held off for now:** the map also shows Greenland, Quebec, and a southern polity currently labeled "the CSA" on the map — the developer flagged that this last name specifically may change, so it's excluded from the signatory list until settled. Greenland and Quebec weren't addressed either way and remain open.
- **The United Assembly of Nations' membership, confirmed 2026-07-08:** effectively universal — the meaningful list is the short one of exclusions, not members. Real-world examples given for the excluded tier: Guam, Papua New Guinea, St. Kitts & Nevis, Tuvalu, Seychelles, Fiji, and similar small nations with negligible international presence and no robot populations of their own. The preamble reflects this by naming a handful of confirmed Upper Earth nations only as illustrative examples ("among them...") rather than as the complete membership — the actual member list would run to the overwhelming majority of Upper Earth's sovereign nations, most of which remain unnamed in Tepenian canon.
