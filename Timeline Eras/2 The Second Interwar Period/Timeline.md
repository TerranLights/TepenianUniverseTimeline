# The Second Interwar Period — Timeline (2564–2812)

The span between the signing of the Falkland Treaty on June 21, 2564 and the Long
Night War, now canonized as the single precise year 2812 — roughly **248 years**.
This document codifies that era using the same composite story-beat structure as
`Worldspace/Locations-and-Levels/Outside-World/Upper-Earth/Timeline.md` (The First
Interwar Period, 2083–2564) — see [[project_first_second_interwar_disambiguation]]
and [[project_interwar_timeline]] for the full source methodology and every beat's
definition, reused verbatim here rather than re-derived:

- **Blake Snyder, *Save the Cat*** — standard beat names and duration percentages,
  the baseline structural skeleton. Drawn from as fully as possible.
- **James Scott Bell, *Plot and Structure*** — "Doorway of No Return" for the two act
  breaks, "Mirror Moment" for the midpoint, plus Kick in the Shins/Care Package/
  Q-Factor/Pet the Dog. Drawn from as fully as possible.
- **John Truby, *The Anatomy of Story*** — character-web concepts (Ally/Allies,
  Opposition, Fake Ally, Moral Decision, Self-Revelation, etc.). Drawn from as fully
  as possible.
- **Joseph Campbell, *The Hero's Journey*** — used selectively: kept only where a
  beat can be reinterpreted into sci-fi form (Apotheosis, Atonement with the Father,
  Meeting with the Goddess) or cross-references cleanly onto a Save the Cat beat
  (Road of Trials = Fun and Games; Refusal of the Call = Debate); dropped where
  irreducibly fantasy-coded (e.g. Magic Flight).

**Important caveat, same as the First Interwar Period file:** in-world dates below are
chosen for what actually happens at each point, not forced to match Save the Cat's
recommended percentages exactly — except where noted below as a deliberate, explicit
placement decision.

**Placeholder end-date, flagged 2026-07-05:** only the year 2812 is canon for the
Long Night War — no exact day is established yet. This file provisionally uses
**June 21, 2812** (mirroring the Falkland Treaty's own day) purely to get a clean
248.0-year span for percentage math. Treat every date below as adjustable once the
war's actual day is settled.

**Seasonal window established 2026-07-05, narrowing (not yet fixing) the exact
day:** the developer wants the war's start date to fall somewhere between **March
22 and June 20** — Southern Hemisphere astronomical autumn, running up to (but not
past) the winter solstice — so that the war is triggered heading *into* the
Antarctic polar night, not during or after it. This is a deliberate thematic choice:
"Long Night War" should describe a war that begins as the literal long night is
arriving, not one that starts once it's already dark. The current June 21 placeholder
sits exactly one day past this window (the solstice itself) and should be moved
into the March 22 – June 20 range once a specific day is chosen — not yet decided
which day within that range.

**Major open tension, flagged 2026-07-05 — not yet resolved:** the developer has
decided Amundsen Tower's completion belongs at this era's **Midpoint**, tentatively
placed at exactly 50% (**June 21, 2688**, under the placeholder span above) — see the
Midpoint section below for the full reasoning. This directly **contradicts** Amundsen
Tower's completion date as already established elsewhere in the GDD (**~2629–2639**,
cited in `Concordia_Second_Interwar_Cultural_Sheet.md`, `TODO.md`'s Infrastructure
Sequence entry, and — most consequentially — the entire "~178-year operating window"
throughput calculation in `Theoretical-Calculations/Amundsen_Tower_Space_Fountain_
Design.md`). The developer's own framing: this is tentative, "somewhere around" the
Midpoint rather than literally 50%, with a conclusive year/day to be settled once
more background is developed. **Until resolved, do not treat either date (~2629-2639
or ~2688) as settled canon — both are live possibilities, and reconciling the Tower's
math file against whichever date wins is a real, tracked task — see `TODO.md`.**

## Beat-by-Beat Detail

### Opening Image
**June 21, 2564** — **Confirmed canon**

The Falkland Treaty is signed. The War of Upper Earth ends; Upper Earth wins; the
anti-robot side prevails. Robots (and their human allies) are exiled to Antarctica,
establishing the Federation of Tepenia. See `World_History_Reference.md` for full
detail. This is simultaneously the END of the First Interwar Period and the Opening
Image of the Second — the same historical event serves as both bookends.

### Break into Two
**~2614**

TBD — the "Doorway of No Return #1." Whatever happens here commits Tepenia's
trajectory irreversibly toward its ascendant nation-building era (per
`Concordia_Second_Interwar_Cultural_Sheet.md`'s established framing of the Second
Interwar Period as the Federation's confident, outward-looking golden age) and,
eventually, toward the Midpoint.

**Combined direction confirmed 2026-07-05 (event/shape still TBD — all three kept
as live candidates, not yet chosen between):** the developer wants Break into Two
built from two combined ingredients — **a crisis that proves isolated cities can't
survive alone** (forcing federation as necessity, not just policy) and **a
generational handoff** (the founding generation giving way to Tepenians born in
exile, with no memory of Upper Earth). The generational angle is especially
well-grounded in existing lore: by ~2614 (50 years post-Treaty), most *human*
founders would be elderly or already dead — human lifespans aren't meaningfully
extended here — while the robots present at the founding are still fully active,
since they live for centuries. This makes robots the one group spanning *both*
generations, uniquely positioned to bridge (or complicate) the handoff. Three
candidate shapes for how this plays out, all kept open for now:

- **Shape A — the crisis discredits isolationist caution outright.** A prolonged
  hardship hits several early cities at once. The founders' instinct (self-reliance,
  forged by the trauma of exile) is for each city to handle it alone; the cities that
  do this nearly collapse. Exile-born humans, without the founders' hard-earned
  caution, simply organize cross-city aid without waiting for permission — and it
  works decisively, discrediting isolationism and creating the political will for
  real federation (shared highways, the Arcanet) that didn't exist before.
- **Shape B — a leadership handoff collides with the crisis by accident.** The
  founders' council (increasingly robot-only as human founders age out or die) is
  already mid-transition, formally handing decision-making to the native-born
  generation, when the crisis hits. The new leadership has no choice but to improvise
  a federated response immediately, under real pressure — Break into Two as
  something the era is thrown into, not a calm policy choice.
- **Shape C — the robots are the ones who push for federation, not the humans.**
  Flips the expected roles: it's the long-lived robots — who remember the founding
  generation's fear clearly, but also have centuries of perspective the newly-adult
  humans don't — who recognize the isolationist instinct has outlived its
  usefulness. Their institutional memory argues for change; the exile-born humans
  are the ones willing to actually act on it. Uses the robots-as-memory-keepers theme
  directly (see [[project_tepenia_demographics_reproduction]]) rather than just
  having them present for the transition.

None of these are mutually exclusive with each other or with additional shapes not
yet identified — kept open deliberately, to be chosen from (or combined further)
once more background/context is developed.

### Road of Trials / Fun and Games / Promise of the Premise
**~2614 – ~2688**

TBD in detail, but per already-established canon (see `Infrastructure Sequence` in
`TODO.md`), this span should cover: cities founded and built out → subnet-internal
highways wired → the Arcanet gradually connected subnet by subnet → Hwy 22 (the
Transcontinental Highway) built, reaching the South Pole/Amundsen Station directly →
only then does Amundsen Tower's own construction begin, in the final ~12–17 years of
this span (per already-established canon on the Tower's own construction timeline).

**B-Story = the Arcanet's construction, established 2026-07-05.** Fits cleanly: the
B-Story starts early in Road of Trials (right after Break into Two, per the general
methodology) and converges with the main plot at the Midpoint — matching the
Arcanet's own gradual, subnet-by-subnet buildout culminating around the same time as
the Tower's completion. Cities are already built out by this point (per the sequence
above, cities come first, during Act 1), so the Arcanet beginning as a quiet,
seemingly-separate infrastructure thread that later proves central (converging with
the Tower/energy-regulation main plot at the Midpoint) is a strong structural fit.

**Highways — not yet placed, per the developer (2026-07-05).** Where subnet-internal
highway construction fits thematically/structurally within this span (or possibly
earlier, during Debate/Break into Two) is still an open question. Given the
established sequence lists highways *before* the Arcanet, this may need to be placed
earlier than the Arcanet/B-Story above, or the sequence itself may need revisiting —
not yet decided either way.

**Resolved 2026-07-05 — Kick in the Shins is NOT Amundsen Tower's construction
start.** The developer initially proposed Tower construction *beginning* at Kick in
the Shins (early Road of Trials, ~2614) but agreed this conflicts with the
already-established Infrastructure Sequence, which has construction beginning only
in the **final ~12-17 years** before the ~2688 Midpoint completion (~2671-2676), not
at the start of this ~74-year span — preserving the material-logistics/
construction-rate math already worked out in `Theoretical-Calculations/Amundsen_
Tower_Space_Fountain_Design.md`. Kick in the Shins needs a **different, genuinely
setback-shaped event** — something that actually goes wrong for Tepenia early in
Road of Trials, not a positive infrastructure milestone.

**Two candidates kept for future consideration, chosen 2026-07-05 from a
four-option brainstorm (not decided between yet; more candidates may be added
later):**
- **The person who made Break into Two's federated response work is lost almost
  immediately after.** Whoever drove the successful cross-city cooperation (the
  crisis response from Break into Two) dies, is badly hurt, or withdraws right when
  everyone assumed the hard part was over — creating real doubt about whether the
  federation spirit survives without that specific person.
- **An early infrastructure failure.** A first real attempt at a highway segment or
  an Arcanet subnet-connection fails outright — an engineering problem, a resource
  shortage, terrain nobody accounted for — a concrete, physical setback showing the
  new ambition outpaced the actual capability, at least at first.

Two other candidates (federation proving harder in ongoing practice than the crisis
made it look; isolationist backlash from a resentful city/faction) were considered
but not carried forward for now.

### Midpoint / Mirror Moment
**June 21, 2688 (tentative)**

**Amundsen Tower's completion** — placed here deliberately, per the developer's
2026-07-05 direction, as a **False Victory** (Snyder's Midpoint is either a False
Victory or a False Defeat; this is unambiguously the former). The Tower's completion
reads as the Federation's greatest achievement — a source of immense national pride,
built largely by robot labor over generations, marking the peak of the ascendant,
nation-building mood established as this era's defining character. It's a "false"
victory in the structural sense that the era's actual ending (the Long Night War) is
still 124 years away — the height of national confidence and infrastructure triumph
precedes, rather than prevents, the eventual catastrophe.

**Reframed 2026-07-05 — this "victory" is bigger, and its later loss more
catastrophic, than initially framed.** Per `Theoretical-Calculations/Amundsen_Tower_
Space_Fountain_Design.md`'s new "Primary Purpose" section, the Tower's actual primary
functions are continent-scale energy regulation (acting as the whole grid's
stabilizing flywheel) and bulk material mass-driving to/from orbit — passenger
transport is a side-application, not the achievement itself. This means the Midpoint
isn't just "the nation built an impressive space elevator" — it's "the nation's
entire energy grid and orbital material economy came fully online," a far larger
foundation for the false victory to rest on, and a far larger, multi-axis
catastrophe when it's lost at END (see `Energy_Grid_Failure_Rationale.md` #11 for the
resulting acute grid-overload event plus permanent chronic instability).

**Tentative placement, not yet reconciled — see the caveat at the top of this file.**
The Tower's completion is established elsewhere as ~2629–2639, not 2688; this
50%-exact placement is a deliberate structural choice pending a final decision on
the actual date, made "somewhere around" the Midpoint rather than locked to exactly
half the era.

**Confirmed span math (2026-07-05):** Midpoint (2688) to Break into Three (~2762,
the Long Night War's inciting incident — see below) is **~74 years**; Break into
Three to END (2812) is **~50 years**. Combined, the Tower operates for roughly
**120 years** between its completion and its destruction in the war — its entire
functional lifetime. Over that window, the Tower's own already-established design
capacity (~194 people/hour) moves ~204 million people — enough to move Tepenia's
entire population (~32M) roughly **6.4 times over**. See
`Theoretical-Calculations/Amundsen_Tower_Space_Fountain_Design.md`'s "Additional
Proposed Functions" section for the full calculation and why this makes developing
the Tower's non-passenger functions a real priority, not just a nice-to-have.

### Dark Night of the Soul
**~2750 – ~2762**

TBD — the aftermath of the low point, leading into the final act break.

**Placement rule (reused from the First Interwar Period methodology):** both Truby's
**Moral Decision** and Bell's **Q-Factor** land somewhere within this span.

**The Merit Board Audit** — **c. 2761, Confirmed canon** (see
`Worldspace/Locations-and-Levels/Concordia-City/Districts/District_Canon_Reference.md`).
An independent review found that Capricorn's merit rating system had systematically
undervalued robot workers' contributions compared to human workers performing
equivalent tasks, for decades. Capricorn's leadership disputed the methodology,
implemented minor adjustments, and quietly buried the original findings — the
findings' political consequences remain unresolved and suppressed at the war's
outbreak. Lands at ~79.2%, right at the tail end of Dark Night of the Soul — Break into Three
itself is now assigned to the Long Night War's inciting incident (~2762, see below),
so the Audit reads most naturally as a Moral Decision-adjacent event within Dark
Night of the Soul: a suppressed truth the Federation is forced to reckon with,
shortly before the killing that triggers the war. **Flagged 2026-07-05: the
developer wants to revisit and likely change a lot about the Merit Board Audit,
explicitly deferred until all cities are developed first — don't treat its current
framing/details as settled; see `TODO.md`.**

### Break into Three
**~2762**

**The Long Night War's inciting incident** — placed here deliberately, per the
developer's 2026-07-05 direction: an Upper Earth diplomat denies a gynoid's
personhood, attempts assault on her in Palmer City, and she kills him in self-defense
(see [[project_long_night_war_inciting_incident]] — her name/fate and his identity
still TBD). This is the "Doorway of No Return #2" — the single event that commits
the era irreversibly toward the Long Night War, with no path back.

### Finale
**~2762 – June 21, 2812**

TBD. This ~50-year span must terminate in the Long Night War itself, the second
anti-robot war (see `World_History_Reference.md`). Given the inciting incident is now
placed at the *start* of this span (Break into Three, ~2762) rather than near its
end, this Finale covers roughly **50 years of escalation** from the initial killing
to the war's actual outbreak — fallout, diplomatic breakdown, rising anti-robot
sentiment on Upper Earth, whatever intervening events widen a single act of
self-defense into a full war. Worth developing that escalation explicitly, since 50
years is a long gap for a single inciting incident to sit unresolved before the
war it caused actually begins — flagged for confirmation, not assumed correct as-is.

**The Flood** — **c. 2771, Confirmed canon** (see `District_Canon_Reference.md`). An
experimental neural interface mod malfunctioned, creating a period during which a
significant portion of Pisces residents experienced involuntary memory sharing —
experienced as spiritual transcendence by some, pure trauma by others. Lands at
~83.3%, early in the Finale span.

### Closing Image
**~99–100%, the final 0–1% of the era**

TBD — no event yet established. Structurally the mirror/bookend of the Opening
Image (the Falkland Treaty being signed): the "after" snapshot of Tepenia
immediately before the Long Night War changes everything again. Given the Long Night
War is itself both this era's END and the Opening Image of whatever comes next (the
same doubling pattern as the First/Second Interwar boundary at 2564), this beat
likely captures Tepenia at its most confident and developed — the peak the war then
destroys.

### END — The Long Night War
**2812 (day TBD, narrowed to March 22 – June 20)** — **Confirmed canon (year only;
seasonal window established 2026-07-05, see top-of-file caveat)**

The Long Night War begins and Tepenia is devastated — see
`World_History_Reference.md` and [[project_district_canon_timeline_fix]] for the
canonization of 2812 as a single precise year. Several cities are destroyed
outright (Zukelli, Belgrano's later ruin, Denison, Palmer City, Casey), others
damaged but functional; the Federation's post-war era (the game's present day,
≈2822–2827) begins in this event's aftermath.

**Two-axis catastrophe, established 2026-07-05.** Amundsen Tower's physical
collapse happens in this same event, and — per its reframed Primary Purpose as the
continent's energy-regulating flywheel — its destruction plausibly triggers a
simultaneous, continent-wide power-grid overload (the real-world "load rejection"
phenomenon: every subnet tied into a grid regulated by the Tower suddenly loses that
regulation at once), independent of and additional to city-by-city physical damage.
This is also the origin point of the grid's *permanent, chronic* instability that
persists into the present day (≈2822–2827) — see `Energy_Grid_Failure_Rationale.md`
#11 for the full mechanism. The war's damage to Tepenia is therefore two distinct
things happening at once: physical destruction of cities and infrastructure, and a
simultaneous electrical catastrophe across the surviving power grid.
